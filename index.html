From 2023 to 2026, overall consumer prices rose steadily, while energy prices followed a more volatile line. The overall CPI climbed from about 300 to over 330, reflecting persistent inflation across the economy.

In contrast, the Energy CPI fluctuated throughout most of the period, falling during parts of 2023 and 2024 before stabilizing in 2025. The highest change occurred in early 2026, when energy prices surged sharply, narrowing the gap with the overall CPI.

This highlights a recurring feature of inflation, while prices for most goods and services tend to increase gradually, energy costs are far more sensitive to shifts in supply, demand, and global market conditions.

According to the Associated Press, This sharp increase in energy prices during early 2026 occured with the escalation of the Iran conflict, which contributed to rising oil prices as markets reacted to the risk of tighter energy supplies.

Due to the shutdown of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical route for global energy shipments. As there was anticipated potential shortages, crude oil prices rose significantly, increasing the cost of gasoline, transportation, and other energy-related goods.

These geopolitical tensions contributed to the surge in the Energy CPI, demonstrating how international events can quickly influence domestic energy prices and amplify inflationary pressures felt by consumers.
