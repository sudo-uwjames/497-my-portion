From 2023 to 2026, overall consumer prices rose steadily, while energy prices followed a more volatile line. The overall CPI climbed from roughly 300 to over 330, reflecting persistent inflation across the economy. In contrast, the Energy CPI fluctuated throughout most of the period, falling during parts of 2023 and 2024 before stabilizing in 2025. The most striking change occurred in early 2026, when energy prices surged sharply, narrowing the gap with the overall CPI. This divergence highlights a recurring feature of inflation, while prices for most goods and services tend to increase gradually, energy costs are far more sensitive to shifts in supply, demand, and global market conditions.
